Google

September 4, 1998, Menlo Park, CA

Google is a multinational technology company specializing in Internet-related services and products. Founded in 1998 by Larry Page and Sergey Brin while they were Ph.D. students at Stanford University, Google initially started as a search engine designed to organize the vast amount of information on the web.


1.Search Engine: Google’s search engine is its most well-known product, dominating the global market with a massive share. It's designed to provide relevant search results quickly by using complex algorithms.
2.Advertising: Google’s advertising services, particularly Google Ads (formerly AdWords) and AdSense, are major revenue sources. These platforms allow businesses to display ads on Google’s search results and other websites.
3.Android: Google develops the Android operating system, the most widely used mobile OS globally. Android powers a vast majority of smartphones and tablets.
4.Cloud Computing: Google Cloud provides a range of cloud services, including computing power, storage, and machine learning tools, catering to businesses of all sizes.
5.YouTube: Acquired by Google in 2006, YouTube is the world’s largest video-sharing platform, offering a vast range of content from user-generated videos to professional productions.
6.Gmail and Productivity Tools: Google offers Gmail, one of the most popular email services, along with other productivity tools like Google Docs, Sheets, and Drive.
7.AI and Machine Learning: Google is heavily invested in artificial intelligence, with applications ranging from Google Assistant to self-driving car technology via its subsidiary, Waymo.
